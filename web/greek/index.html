Greek States of America

Mapping hotspots for Greek colleges and universities.

Sororities ⇓


Hover over the dots to view the school and percentage of undergraduates in fraternities and sororities.

These heat maps display data from the US News data on schools with the most students in fraternities and sororities. Why a heat map? A heat map was chosen because my (unscientific) and anecdotal assumption is that high involvement in greek life at colleges is associated with cultures that bleed into life outside of and after school. Therefore, clusters of schools with lots of greek life presumably influence the culture of the surrounding area.

*The heat maps were generated in QGIS with the heatmap plugin, which uses a kernel density estimation to create a 'heat' raster from point data. In this case, the point data were cut off at a lower limit of 20% undergraduates in fraternities/sororities and were weighted by percentage, so that schools with high percentages have more 'heat'. The output is an image that visualizes clusters. Point data and the web maps were stylized in MapBox's TileMill .

I understand there are limitations to my methods, data, and assumptions for these maps. Just one example, small schools with very high percentages may not be as influential as large schools with lower percentage. More for someone else to explore!
